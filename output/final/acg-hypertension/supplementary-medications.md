# Supplementary Table S1 – BP-lowering Medications

This table only includes medications from the major classes mentioned in the ACG and is non-exhaustive. Refer to local product inserts for full details before prescribing. Clinical judgement should be exercised at all times when making decisions for an individual patient. For fixed-dose combination products, refer to information on individual components.

> **Note:** Maximum dosage may be lower for elderly. Underlined medications in the source PDF denote availability on Singapore government subsidy list (subsidy indicators not reproduced here). "Use not recommended" indicates limited or no clinical data reported in the product information leaflets.

## ACE Inhibitors

Mechanism: inhibits the formation of angiotensin II, leading to vasodilation.

Precautions: women of child-bearing potential. Contraindication: pregnant women.
Common side effects: decline in renal function, hyperkalaemia, angioedema, cough (more common with ACE inhibitors than ARBs).

| Name | Initial dose | Max dose | Renal dose adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| captopril | 6.25–25 mg BD-TDS | 50 mg TDS | CrCl 10–50: 75% of normal dose every 12–18h; CrCl <10: 50% of normal dose every 24h |
| enalapril | 2.5–10 mg OD | 40 mg/day | CrCl 10–30: initial 2.5 mg/day in 1–2 divided doses, max 20 mg/day; CrCl <10: initial 1.25 mg OD or 2.5 mg EOD, max 10 mg/day |
| imidapril | 2.5–10 mg OD | 20 mg OD | Renal impairment: initial 2.5 mg OD; CrCl <30: consider halving dose or prolonging interval |
| lisinopril | 2.5–10 mg OD | 40 mg OD | CrCl <10: initial 2.5 mg OD; CrCl 10–30: initial 2.5–5 mg OD |
| perindopril arginine | 2.5–5 mg OM | 10 mg OD | CrCl 60–89: max 5 mg/day; CrCl 30–59: max 2.5 mg/day; CrCl 15–29: max 2.5 mg EOD |
| perindopril erbumine | 4 mg OD | 8–16 mg OD | CrCl 30–80: initial 2 mg OM, max 8 mg OM; CrCl <30: not recommended |
| ramipril | 2.5 mg OD | 10 mg/day | CrCl 20–50: initial 1.25 mg OM, max 5 mg/day |

## ARBs

Mechanism: blocks type 1 angiotensin II receptors, preventing vascular contraction.

Side effects: similar to ACE inhibitors, but persistent dry cough less likely.

| eprosartan | 300–600 mg OM | 1200 mg OD | CrCl <60: initial 300 mg OM |
| fimasartan | 60 mg OD | 120 mg OD | CrCl <30: initial 30 mg OD, max 60 mg OD |
| irbesartan | 75–150 mg OD | 300 mg OD | No dosage adjustment needed |
| losartan | 25–50 mg OD | 100 mg OD | CrCl <20: initial 25 mg OD |
| olmesartan | 20 mg OD | 40 mg OD | No dose adjustment needed |
| telmisartan | 20–40 mg OD | 80 mg OD | No dose adjustment needed |

## Beta Blockers

Mechanism: blocks norepinephrine and epinephrine from binding to receptors.

Precautions: avoid abrupt discontinuation, risk of bradycardia.
Common side effects: masking of hypoglycaemia, bronchospasm (especially non-cardioselective).

### Cardioselective

| Name | Initial dose | Max dose | Renal dose adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| acebutolol | 100–200 mg BD (200–400 mg/day) | 600 mg BD (1200 mg/day) | CrCl <50: reduce daily dose by 50%; CrCl <25: reduce by 75% |
| atenolol | 25 mg OD-BD | 100 mg/day | CrCl 15–35: max 50 mg OD; CrCl <15: max 25 mg OD or 50 mg EOD |
| bisoprolol | 2.5–5 mg OD | 20 mg/day | CrCl <20: lower initial dose, max 10 mg/day |
| metoprolol tartrate | 50 mg BD | 400 mg/day in divided doses | No dose adjustment needed |
| nebivolol | 2.5–5 mg OD | 40 mg/day (>65 years: max 5 mg/day) | Renal insufficiency: initial 2.5 mg OD, max 5 mg OD |

### Non-cardioselective

| Name | Initial dose | Max dose | Renal dose adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| carvedilol | 12.5 mg OD for first 2 days, then 25 mg OD | 50 mg/day | No dose adjustment needed |
| labetalol | 100 mg BD | 800–2400 mg/day in divided doses | No dose adjustment needed |

## CCBs

Mechanism: prevents calcium from entering the cells of the heart and arteries, reducing contraction of arteries and allowing vasodilation.

Precautions: hepatic impairment.
Common side effects: oedema, dizziness, headache.

### Dihydropyridine

| Name | Initial dose | Max dose | Renal dose adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| amlodipine | 5 mg OD | 10 mg OD | No dose adjustment needed |
| felodipine | 2.5–5 mg OD | 20 mg OD | — |
| lacidipine | 2 mg OM | 6 mg OM | — |
| nifedipine LA | 20–30 mg OD | 120 mg OD | — |

### Non-dihydropyridine

Precautions: avoid abrupt discontinuation, heart block, LV dysfunction, hepatic impairment.
Additional side effects: bradycardia, constipation (verapamil), hepatotoxicity.

| Name | Initial dose | Max dose | Renal dose adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| verapamil (IR) | 40–80 mg TDS | 360–480 mg in divided doses | Use with caution in renal impairment |

| diltiazem (IR) | 30–60 mg TDS | — | — |
| diltiazem (SR, BD) | 90 mg BD | — | — |
| diltiazem (SR, OD) | 100–200 mg OD | — | — |

Note: verapamil immediate release is not registered in Singapore but available as exemption item.

## Diuretics

Mechanism: diminishes sodium reabsorption at different sites in the nephron, increasing urinary sodium and water loss.

Precautions: risk of squamous cell carcinoma.
Common side effects: hyperuricaemia, hypokalaemia, hyponatraemia, hypercalcaemia, hyperglycaemia, photosensitivity.

### Thiazide and thiazide-like

| Name | Initial dose | Max dose | Renal dose adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| hydrochlorothiazide | 12.5–25 mg OD | 50–100 mg OD | Use with caution; CrCl <10: not recommended (lack of efficacy) |
| indapamide (IR) | 2.5 mg OM | 2.5 mg OM | CrCl <30: contraindicated |
| indapamide (SR) | 1.5 mg OM | 1.5 mg OM | CrCl <30: contraindicated |

Dosing abbreviations: BD = twice daily; EOD = every other day; OD = once daily; OM = once every morning; TDS = three times daily; IR = immediate release; LA = long acting; SR = slow release; CrCl = creatinine clearance in mL/min.
