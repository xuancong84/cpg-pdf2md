## Key Recommendations

| # | Recommendation | Section |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Include cardiovascular risk assessment to inform management options for patients with elevated BP | [Diagnosis and Risk](./diagnosis-and-risk.md) |
| 2 | Use an ACE inhibitor, ARB, or CCB as first-line; consider thiazide/thiazide-like diuretics as alternative first-line if indicated | [Pharmacotherapy Selection](./pharmacotherapy-selection.md) |
| 3 | Avoid initiating beta blockers as first-line monotherapy unless favourable effect on comorbidities expected | [Pharmacotherapy Selection](./pharmacotherapy-selection.md) |
| 4 | Consider initiating low-dose dual therapy from two different classes based on required BP reduction and CV risk | [Combination Therapy](./combination-therapy.md) |
| 5 | Intensify antihypertensive medications if response to initial treatment not achieved (~3 months) | [Treatment Intensification](./treatment-intensification.md) |
| 6 | Follow up all patients with hypertension at least every six months, with more frequent review as needed | [Specialist Referral](./specialist-referral.md) |
