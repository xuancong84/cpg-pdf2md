# Supplementary Table S2 – Single-pill Combinations

## CCBs

Mechanism: prevents calcium from entering the cells of the heart and arteries, reducing contraction of arteries and allowing vasodilation.

Precautions: hepatic impairment.
Common side effects: oedema, dizziness, headache.

### Dihydropyridine

| Name | Initial dose | Max dose | Renal dose adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| amlodipine | 5 mg OD | 10 mg OD | No dose adjustment needed |
| felodipine | 2.5–5 mg OD | 20 mg OD | — |
| lacidipine | 2 mg OM | 6 mg OM | — |
| nifedipine LA | 20–30 mg OD | 120 mg OD | — |

### Non-dihydropyridine

Precautions: avoid abrupt discontinuation, heart block, LV dysfunction, hepatic impairment.
Additional side effects: bradycardia, constipation (verapamil), hepatotoxicity.

| Name | Initial dose | Max dose | Renal dose adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| verapamil (IR) | 40–80 mg TDS | 360–480 mg in divided doses | Use with caution in renal impairment |
| verapamil (SR) | 120–240 mg OM | 480 mg OM | Use with caution in renal impairment |
| diltiazem (IR) | 30–60 mg TDS | — | — |
| diltiazem (SR, BD) | 90 mg BD | — | — |
| diltiazem (SR, OD) | 100–200 mg OD | — | — |

Note: verapamil immediate release is not registered in Singapore but available as exemption item.

## Diuretics

Mechanism: diminishes sodium reabsorption at different sites in the nephron, increasing urinary sodium and water loss.

Precautions: risk of squamous cell carcinoma.
Common side effects: hyperuricaemia, hypokalaemia, hyponatraemia, hypercalcaemia, hyperglycaemia, photosensitivity.

### Thiazide and thiazide-like

| Name | Initial dose | Max dose | Renal dose adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| hydrochlorothiazide | 12.5–25 mg OD | 50–100 mg OD | Use with caution; CrCl <10: not recommended (lack of efficacy) |
| indapamide (IR) | 2.5 mg OM | 2.5 mg OM | CrCl <30: contraindicated |
| indapamide (SR) | 1.5 mg OM | 1.5 mg OM | CrCl <30: contraindicated |
